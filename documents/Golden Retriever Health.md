# Golden Retriever Health Overview

Golden Retrievers are a popular breed known for their friendly temperament and intelligence, but like all breeds, they face specific health challenges. Understanding these helps owners provide better care and seek early interventions.

## Common Health Issues

1. **Hip and Elbow Dysplasia**  
   One of the most prevalent health problems in Golden Retrievers, this skeletal disorder involves abnormal development of the ball-and-socket joints, leading to pain and mobility issues.

2. **Hypothyroidism**  
   This endocrine disorder, often occurring post-spaying/neutering, leads to reduced thyroid hormone production which can affect metabolism and energy levels.

3. **Cancer**  
   Unfortunately, Golden Retrievers are predisposed to several types of cancer, which significantly impact their life expectancy.

4. **Skin and Eye Conditions**  
   Issues like allergies, dermatitis, and eye diseases can affect this breed.

5. **Respiratory Problems and Heart Disease**

6. **Congenital Hypomyelinating Polyneuropathy**  
   A genetic nerve disorder causing weakness and slow motor responses.

## Causes and Risk Factors

- Genetic predisposition plays a significant role. Screening and responsible breeding practices have been developed to reduce inherited disorders.
- Spaying/neutering has been linked with higher risks of hypothyroidism and pancreatitis in females.

## Health Management and Prevention

- **Genetic Testing:** Screening for over 230 genetic health risks via dog DNA tests can help prospective owners and breeders.
- **Regular Veterinary Care:** Includes routine physical exams, blood tests (e.g., thyroid hormone levels), and screenings.
- **Diet and Exercise:** Maintaining healthy weight to prevent joint stress.
- **Selecting Responsible Breeders:** Ensuring health screenings are done reduces risk of inherited diseases.

## Life Expectancy

Generally around 10-12 years, with health challenges like cancer and joint diseases being primary factors affecting longevity.
